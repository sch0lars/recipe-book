Hambagu (Japanese Hamburger Steak)

Ingredients

Patties

  • 1/2 onion, minced
  • 1 Tbsp neutral oil
  • 2 lb ground beef and pork combination
  • 1/2 tsp Diamond Crystal kosher salt
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/3 cup panko (Japanese breadcrumbs)
  • 2 Tbsp milk
  • 1 large egg

Tonkatsu Sauce

  • 4 1/2 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 Tbsp sugar (plus more to taste)
  • 5 Tbsp ketchup
  • 2 Tbsp oyster sauce

Directions

  1. Heat a large pan over medium heat. When the pan is hot, add 1 Tbsp neutral oil. Then, add the onions.
  2. Saute the onions until tender and almost translucent. Transfer to a large bowl and let it cool.
  3. Once the onions are cool, mix in ground beef and pork blend, salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
  4. Mix in the panko, milk, and egg, then knead the mixture until it is sticky.
  5. Roll the mixture into balls and then flatten into an oval. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  6. Add 1 Tbsp neutral oil to a large pan and place the patties. Create indentations in the middle of each patty.
  7. Cook each side until browned. Set patties aside.
  8. Meanwhile, in a small bowl, combine Worcestershire, ketchup, sugar, and oyster sauce to make tonkatsu sauce.
  9. Pour tonkatsu sauce over patties when serving. Serve with steamed rice.
